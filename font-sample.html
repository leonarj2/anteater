Pangrams

  1. The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.
  2. Sphinx of black quartz, judge my vow.
  3. Jinxed wizards pluck ivy from the big quilt.
  4. Jaded zombies acted quaintly but kept driving their oxen forward.

The word pangrams comes from the Greek root words pan, meaning "all," and gram, which means "something written or recorded."
